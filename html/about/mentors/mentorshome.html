Celia Cantabranda

Dance

Ms. Cantabrana began her journey with the Si Se Puede Foundation at the age of six. She began as a student of the ballet folklorico dance program where she learned various traditional Mexican dances from a number of dance instructors.

She has always believed in the SSPF's vision and hopes children who attend its programs, return to help future children. Ms. Cantabrana currently attends Mesa Community College where she is part of the National Honors Society of Collegiate Scholars and plans to attend Arizona State University for a BA in Cultural Studies.

Celia can be reached at sisepuede.dance@gmail.com.

Greg Lewis

Calculus & Robotics

Greg has volunteered with Si Se Puede Foundation since 2007 mainly with the Robotics club but also with the Scholars group and Calculus competitions.

He recently retired from Intel Corp. where he worked 27 years in various roles in Assembly/Test/Technology Development and ended his career in Corporate Planning and Logistics. His inspiration for working with these kids is to pay it forward for all of the opportunities he was given. For this he was awarded the Intel 2015 International Hero of the year and the Chandler Gilbert Community College Teal & Silver Community Partner award.

Congratulations to Mr. Greg Lewis, for being selected as Intel's "2015 International Hero of the Year". Mr. Lewis, was honored for his eight years of service and 1000's of hours donated to SSPF's STEM Programs. We salute Mr. Lewis for his dedication.
