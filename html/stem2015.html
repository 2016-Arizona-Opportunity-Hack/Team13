2015 STEM Conference

On Friday November 6, 2015, Si Se Puede Foundation, in partnership with South Mountain Community College, hosted the inaugural STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics) Workshop on the campus of SMCC. The purpose of this first-time event was to expose two-hundred seventy, ninth grade students from Betty Fairfax, Cesar Chavez and Carl Hayden High Schools, to the hands-on discovery and study of STEM. This event also served as a recruitment effort to attract students to participate in the SSPF STEM/Robotics program to be delivered to underserved communities in South Phoenix beginning August 2016. The workshops included classes in Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Technology, Computer Coding and Mathematics. Also included was an exhibit area where Lego Robots, Underwater Remote Operated Vehicle (ROV), and a Mechanical Robot were on display. Discussions of each category of robot were conducted explaining the purpose, construction and operation of the robot.
